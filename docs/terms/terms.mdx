## 3. Access Restrictions and Prohibitions

Access to this system is granted on a strictly individualized basis and operates on the principle of least privilege. Your credentials are a digital reflection of your identity and your specific security clearance within the organization.

### 3.1. Absolute Prohibition on Credential Sharing
Handing over your credentials—including your username, password, biometric access, or two-factor authentication (2FA) codes—to **anyone** is strictly and permanently prohibited. This restriction includes, but is not limited to:
- Colleagues within the same team or department
- Managers, supervisors, or organizational leaders
- Friends, family members, or acquaintances
- IT support personnel (who will never ask for your password)

You are the sole authorized user of your account. If another individual requires access, they must submit a formal request through proper administrative channels. Any detection of shared usage will result in the permanent ban of the compromised identity.

### 3.2. Outsider Access Restrictions
Under no circumstances should individuals outside the organization ("outsiders") be allowed to view, access, or interact with this system. Ensuring this involves strict physical and digital measures:
- You must physically lock your screen whenever you step away from your workstation, even for a moment.

## 4. Network Security and Session Anomalies

To maintain the absolute integrity of our zero-trust architecture, the system employs advanced anomaly detection algorithms designed to neutralize threats before they materialize.

### 4.1. Universal Ban on Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) and Proxies
The use of unauthorized Virtual Private Networks (VPNs), proxy servers, Tor nodes, seedboxes, or any IP-masking services while connecting to the Internal Command Center is **strictly and permanently banned**. Our infrastructure requires accurate, unaltered geolocation and IP tracking to recursively verify the authenticity of your connection requests. Attempting to mask your identity, even for benign reasons, will trigger an automatic, immediate security lock-out and require a formal IT security review.

### 4.2. Strict Multi-Session and Geolocation Restrictions
Concurrent sessions originating from geographically distinct locations are a severe security red flag indicating potential credential compromise or unauthorized credential handover. 
- **The Protocol:** The system is programmed to actively ban and immediately terminate all sessions if multiple logins are detected from different geographic locations simultaneously. 
- **Example Violation:** If an active session is established in New York, and a secondary login is attempted from London moments later, both sessions are terminated, the account is disabled, and an investigation is launched. 
You may only be logged into the platform from a single approved device in a single physical location at any given time.

### 5.2. Prohibition on Unauthorized Security Testing
While we secure our systems diligently, unauthorized penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, or exploiting known/unknown bugs (zero-days) on this platform by team members is explicitly forbidden and illegal under various cybercrime laws. 
- **Reporting Vulnerabilities:** If you discover a vulnerability, a misconfiguration, or an exposed endpoint during your normal duties, you must **cease interaction immediately** and report it to the Security Operations Center (SOC). Attempting to "test" or "verify" the vulnerability yourself will be treated as an exploit attempt.

### 5.3. Immutable Audit Trails
